Your B2 Roadmap
You have a strong foundation from your 5 months in Mexico and B1 certification. At B2 you'll read complex news fluently, understand implied meaning, and express nuanced opinions. Key focus areas: expanding formal register vocabulary, mastering the subjunctive, passive voice, and reported speech.
Daily Routine (30 min)
10 min — Read one article in this app, clicking every highlighted word
10 min — Play a vocab game (aim for 80%+ accuracy)
10 min — Listen to a Spanish podcast (Radio Ambulante is ideal for your level)

B2 Grammar Targets
Present Subjunctive · Imperfect Subjunctive · Conditional Perfect · Passive with se · Reported Speech · Por vs Para (advanced) · Relative Clauses (que / el cual / cuyo)
Mexican Spanish vs News Spanish
Your Mexican ear is a real asset! Be aware that news Spanish is more neutral. Watch for vosotros forms in Spanish outlets, and note that formal news writing uses usted consistently. Spanish news may use leísmo (using le instead of lo for masculine direct objects).
Vocabulary Strategy
At B2, focus on word families, not just single words. When you save desarrollar (to develop), also learn el desarrollo, el desarrollador, desarrollado/a. The Fill-in-the-Blank game is especially good for cementing form-to-meaning links.
